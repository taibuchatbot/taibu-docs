# How to use your Taibu AI OS

Welcome. This is not a chatbot. It is your personal AI operating system.
It knows your business, remembers your decisions, and does real work for
you. This page shows you how to use it. It takes 5 minutes to read.

## 1. What you are looking at

- **Left side**: your project files. Everything the AI knows about you
  lives in these files. You can open and edit any of them.
- **Middle**: the chat. This is where you work. Talk to it like you
  would talk to a smart assistant who can also DO things, not just answer.
- You don't need to be technical. Plain language works. In your own words,
  in your own language.

## 2. Day 1: run the onboarding

Type `/onboard` in the chat, or click the button.

The AI asks you 7 questions: who you are, what you sell, what your
priorities are, which tools you use, and how you write. Then it sets
everything up by itself.

One rule: when it asks for writing samples, **paste real emails or posts
you wrote earlier**. Don't write new ones in the chat. It needs your real
voice, not your "trying to sound good" voice.

After onboarding, try this: ask *"What should I focus on this week?"*
You'll see the difference immediately. It answers using YOUR priorities.

## 3. Everyday use: just ask

Some things people ask on a normal day:

- *"Draft a reply to this email"* (paste the email)
- *"Summarize this document and give me 3 action points"*
- *"What did we decide about pricing last month?"*
- *"Make an offer for a client who wants X"*
- *"Build me a simple overview of my week"*

The AI can read and edit your project files, search the web, and work
with any tools you have connected. If you're not sure whether it can do
something, ask it. The worst case is "no, but here's what I'd need."

## 4. Connect your tools

The AI becomes really useful when it can reach your real data: email,
calendar, files, accounting, CRM.

To connect something, just say so in the chat:

- *"Connect my Gmail"*
- *"Connect Google Calendar"*
- *"I want you to see my Pipedrive"*

It explains the steps and does the setup with you. Some tools connect
with a couple of clicks. Others need an API key. The AI will tell you
exactly where to get it and which line to put it on in the `.env` file.

**Important: never paste API keys into the chat.** Keys go directly into
the `.env` file. The AI knows this rule and will stop you if you forget.

## 5. Weekly rituals: this is where it compounds

Two built-in habits keep your system growing. People who skip these end
up with an expensive chatbot. People who keep them end up with an
operating system.

**`/audit` is your weekly checkup (from day 7).**
It scores your setup on the 4 pillars: Context, Connections, Capabilities,
Habit. You get a score out of 100 and the 3 highest-leverage things to
fix. Run it weekly. Watching the score climb is the point.

**`/level-up` is the Friday ritual (from day 14).**
A short interview that finds ONE thing you did manually this week that
the AI could take over, and then builds it. One week, one automation.
After a couple of months you have a machine working for you.

The app reminds you when these are due. Don't skip them.

## 6. Grow it: new skills

A "skill" is an ability your AI has learned. A repeatable way of doing
a task well. Your OS ships with several. You can add more:

**Find ready-made skills.** Type `/find-skills` and describe what you
need: *"find a skill for writing invoices"*. It searches the skills
ecosystem and installs what fits.

**Turn work you just did into a skill.** This is the most powerful habit
in this whole app. Whenever the AI just did something well, say:

> *"Great. Save that as a skill so we do it the same way next time."*

It writes down the method, and from now on that task is one command.
Did it produce a good offer? A good weekly report? A good reply to a
complaint? Make each one a skill. That's how your OS gets smarter every
week. And it stays yours.

## 7. Small tips

- **New chat per topic.** Use the + next to the chat tab. It keeps things focused.
- **Attach files** with the paperclip: screenshots, PDFs, spreadsheets.
- **It remembers.** Decisions go to the decisions log, context into files.
  If something important happened, say *"note this down."*
- **Ask for a dashboard.** *"Build me a dashboard of my ad results"* and
  it appears in this panel, under Dashboards.
- **Stuck?** Ask the AI itself: *"How should I use you for X?"* It knows
  this whole system and will point you the right way.
