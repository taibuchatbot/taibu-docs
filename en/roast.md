# /roast — test an idea before building it

Claude's default is to agree with you. `/roast` is the opposite. It convenes a five-persona council that tears your idea apart from every angle and builds it back up. At the end, the Judge gives one honest verdict: **GO / RESHAPE / KILL** — plus the cheapest test to validate the idea before you build.

Use it **before** you spend time and money building the wrong thing.

## When to use it

- Before building a new product, service, or feature
- When weighing a new business direction or pricing model
- When you're too attached to an idea to see its weak spots
- When you want an honest second opinion, not validation

## When NOT to use it

- When the idea is already validated and you're just executing
- For small, reversible decisions (it's easier to just try)

## How to use it

Type in the chat:

```
/roast
```

Then describe your idea in a sentence or two. Claude asks a few clarifying questions (who it's for, how it makes money, what your edge is, what your constraints are), then convenes the council.

## Who's on the council

1. **The Contrarian** — assumes the idea fails. Finds the fatal flaws and the fastest way it dies.
2. **The Expansionist** — makes the strongest case for it. Finds the 10x version and adjacent opportunities.
3. **The Logician** — reasons from first principles: does the mechanism and the math even work?
4. **The Researcher** — brings real-world evidence: competitors, market size, what comparable products charge.
5. **The Buyer** — role-plays your exact target customer. Would they actually pay? What's their real objection?

## What you get

- **One clear verdict**: GO, RESHAPE, or KILL — with the reasoning
- **The biggest risk** and **the biggest upside**
- **A money read**: rough price and time to first dollar
- **The cheapest 48-hour test** — the smallest thing to validate the riskiest assumption before building

The most valuable part is that cheap test. It's how you find out if you're right without building the whole thing.
